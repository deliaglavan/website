About Me

Hello! My name is Delia Glavan, I was born and raised in Romania and currently I'm a student at Pace University in New York, double-majoring in Political Science and Peace and Justice Studies, with minors in Theatre for Social Justice and Diplomacy.

As my fields of study are probably able to tell you, I'm interested in seeing how art and politics are intimately connected, and how we can pursue that to further social justice causes.

Experience in Romania

Ever since I've known myself, I've been a very active person through organizing with the people around me. In middle school and high school, that looked like the Student Representation Movement - from the school level to the national one, then the European one, and finally, a global one through UNESCO's SDG4 Youth and Student Network.

Experience in the United States

After moving to New York, I got involved in work within the UN. As part of UNESCO's SDG4 Youth and Student Network, I moderated a panel entitled "Driving accountability and leadership: Accelerating gender equality in and through education," as part of the 68th Commission on the Status of Women.

Last year, I became interested in disarmament causes, which is also what led me to intern for the Meeting of State Parties to the Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons (TPNW) last March, as well as become a member of the Youth for TPNW.

Last summer, I interned for Immigration Equality, the nation's leading LGBTQ and HIV-positive immigrant rights organization.

In the present, I'm interning with Peace Boat US, international NGO that works to build a culture of peace and sustainability around the world by connecting people across borders and creating opportunities for learning, advocacy and cooperation.

Currently, I organize in New York City and on my university campus for the cause of Palestinian liberation and the right to determination of the Palestinian people. I also volunteer with Feminist Collages, organization that urges to raise awareness about femicides happening around the world.

Although these may all sound very different, I fully believe all of our struggles share a lot of common ground, and our solutions are interconnected.
